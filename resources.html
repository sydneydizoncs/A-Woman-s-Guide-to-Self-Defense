CRIMES AGAINST WOMEN

The statistics of crime against women is very alarming -- especially because many are due to a domestic partner.

  • Crime Victims by Gender: Learn more about the statistics of crimes in America by gender.
  • Victims of Sexual Assault: Women all across America unfortunately suffer through an abundance of sexual assault cases every year. Read about the real statistics of sexual assault here.
  • Femicide: Femicide is the murder of women purely because they are women.

DOMESTIC VIOLENCE

Domestic Violence can be mental or physical, and is a problem in many American households, with many abusers going unpunished.

  • Domestic Violence and Physical Abuse: This spreadsheet gives information about abuse linked to domestic settings.
  • The National Intimate Partner and Sexual Violence Survey: The NISVS is a survey that is currently collecting data on domestic violence, sexual violence and stalking crimes in the U.S..
  • Domestic Abuse Hotline: The Domestic Abuse Hotline is a number that you call in cases of domestic violence. There are specific hotlines that you can use if you are afraid of being monitored.

Other Assault Statistics

Abuse comes in all forms, in ways that are unexpected and not stereotypical.

  • Dating and Teen Abuse: Abuse can happen even in non-commital relationships and as teenagers.
  • Abuse in Later Life: Even adults and the elderly undergo abuse.
  • Emotional Abuse: Emotional abuse can happen as well; abuse is not solely physical.

Organizations for Victims

These organizations help victims of abuse or trauma heal and talk about their traumatic event.

  • RAINN: RAINN is the nation's largest anti-sexual violence organization.
  • Domestic Violence Organizations: This is a list of various national domestic violence organizations country-wide.
