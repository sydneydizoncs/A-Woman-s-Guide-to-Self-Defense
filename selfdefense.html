A Guide to Self-Defense

The Basics of Self-Defense

Self-Defense is a fully legal and useful way to defend yourself in case of an agravated assault, and there are many places country-wide that offer trainings, as well as martial arts that also bring about the same benefit.

Some ways to defend youself physically are to:

  • Push back and BE LOUD. Attackers do not want to draw attentiuon in fear of getting caught, so being loud and resistant will possibly cause them to flee, and even if not, will grab the attention of others.
  • Learn about the most effective body parts to hit, such as the pelvis, chin, eyes, nose, and knees. Target these body parts with your fingers, fist, elbow or knee and strike as hard as you can.
  • Use everyday objects to strike them, such as keys, pens, or even throw dirt or sand in their eyes. Women can spray perfume or hairspray as well.
  • Leverage your weight in an atack, as no matter your weight, size or strength, using your weight strategically call allow an advantage over your opponent.
